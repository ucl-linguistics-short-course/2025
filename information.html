About the Programme

UCL Linguistics Short Courses is a training programme organised by UCL Linguistics PhD students, aimed at introducing students of all levels to a broad range of linguistics topics. With even more modules this year, we're covering a wider range of topics like Syntax, Semantics, Pragmatics, Morphology, and Psycholinguistics.

These 5-session courses will run across all three terms and are free to attend and open to everyone. Whether you're accompanying your studies or just looking to dive deeper into these fascinating areas, we've got you covered.

How to Participate

1. Join our mailing list: Email chenjia.gao.18@ucl.ac.uk to receive updates about course schedules and registration.

2. Register for courses: Registration details will be sent via the mailing list before each term begins.

3. Attend sessions: All courses are free and open to everyone. Sessions are held either in-person or online.

4. Receive your certificate: Upon completion of each 5-session course, you'll receive a certificate of participation via email.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can attend these courses?

Our courses are open to everyone, regardless of background or level of experience in linguistics. Whether you're a complete beginner or have some familiarity with the subject, you're welcome to join.

Do I need to attend all sessions?

While we encourage full participation to get the most out of each course, we understand scheduling conflicts may arise. Certificates of participation are typically awarded to those who attend the majority of sessions.

Are the courses recorded?

Recording policies may vary by course. Please check with individual instructors or refer to course-specific announcements sent via the mailing list.

How do I get a certificate?

Certificates of participation are automatically sent to individual emails upon completion of each course. Make sure you're registered with the correct email address.

Can I suggest a topic for a future course?

Absolutely! We welcome suggestions. Please email us at chenjia.gao.18@ucl.ac.uk with your ideas.
